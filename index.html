Types of Wedding

We provide every type of wedding based on your wishes and religion.Join us to get a next level experience.

  • Hindu Wedding

    The Hindu Wedding Ceremony unites two souls spiritually, mentally and physically. The bond of matrimony is sacred and the ceremony of marriage is conducted according to Vedic traditions. The ceremony will be conducted in the ancient Hindu language of Sanskrit and translated to English by the priest.

  • Parsi Wedding

    Parsis ha sa long history in India, and yet they have successfully preserved their culture and traditions. At the same time they have picked up certain local customs which have been incorporated into their wedding rituals. On the whole, Parsi weddings are an interesting mixture of cultures. The weddings itself is an elaborate and tradition-laden affair spanning for several days. Weddings within the Parsi community are known as ‘Lagan’ and they take place at the Parsi Agyari or Fire Temple.

  • Islamic Wedding

    The Muslim wedding, also known as the Nikah ceremony, is one of the most celebrated occasions in Islam because marriage is a Prophetic tradition. With more than 1.9 billion Muslims around the world, the traditions followed at a wedding can differ greatly depending on families, sects, and cultures but the fundamentals of the Nikah ceremony and getting married as a Muslim are always the same.

  • Gujarati Wedding

    These wedding turn utterly musical, with the combination of various musical instruments such as Dhol, Nagada, Turi, Taturi, Murli and so on. The diverse rituals include, Chandlo Matli, which is the beginning of the holy union, Gol Dhana or the engagement ceremony, Mandap Mahurat, Griha Shanti, followed by the buoyant Mehndi and the joyous Sanjhi or Sangeet Sandhya, Pithi, which is the counterpart of Haldi Ceremony in Gujaratui Ritual. The Mosalu and Mameru, Varghodo, Ponkvu, Jaimala, Madhuparka, Antarpaat, and the emotional Kanyadaan, Hista Milap, Mangal Pheras, finally Saptapadi and Sindoor. The vibrancy is continued by post- wedding rituals of Ghar Nu Laxmi and Akei Beki .

  • Punjabi and Sikh Wedding

    In India, many of the Punjabi embrace Sikh culture and reflect its beautiful traditions not only within their daily lives, but within life’s big moments as well- such as weddings, for example. This rich culture revolves around a belief that states the fastest way to a full and peaceful life is through giving and receiving love. Despite the fact that Sikhism boasts some of the most impressive warriors in Indian history, this culture also holds marriage and family in extremely high esteem. This is because such bonds are seen as the key to being brought ever closer to Divine Love.

  • South Indian Wedding

    Nishchitartham, Lagnam, Kashi Yatra or Kanyadaan.. there are over twelve different kinds of South Indian wedding traditions observed in India. Some rituals are common across communities, whereas some traditions are absolutely unique. Each state has its own cultural flavour. Other than the wedding ceremonies, all these states have various kinds of bridal and grooms wear, food, music and ethnicities at different stages of a wedding.

  • Maharastrian Wedding

    Maharashtrian marriage is perhaps the most plain sailing and the least grandiose in the whole country. There are no unnecessary pre-wedding events that have no spiritual significance and the wedding rituals exhibit the core values of the Maharashtrian culture. Yet it is not to be mistaken as this drab and formal affair. Marathi weddings are full of colors and fun rituals that are sure to spice the whole event.

  • Christian Wedding

    The wedding functions start off from the bridal shower, a gift-giving party held for a bride-to-be in anticipation of her wedding. Friends and relatives give gifts to the bride. The bride cuts the cake and the girl who would get the cake with a hidden thimble is supposed to get married next. In a similar pattern, the groom organizes a bachelor's party. After the bridal shower, it's time for Mehendi ceremony. Yes, Christian wedding in India does have Mehendi ceremony. Christian brides and bride get a beautiful mehndi art done on their hands before the wedding. The mehendi ceremony is followed by Haldaat ceremony, during which turmeric and sandalwood paste is applied on both, the bride and groom.
